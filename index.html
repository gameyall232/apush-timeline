July 29, 1955

President Eisenhowever endorses the International Geophysical Year proposed launch of small earth-orbiting satellites, the first of their kind.

Soviet Union launches first Earth-orbiting satellite

October 4, 1957

The Soviet Union successfully launches Sputnik 1 into low earth orbit, the event considered to be the start of the space race.

US launches Explorer 1 into medium earth orbit

January 31, 1958

The United States joins the space race with the launch of their first satellite. The satellite contained more scientific equipment than Sputnik 1.

https://nssdc.gsfc.nasa.gov/nmc/spacecraft/displayTrajectory.action?id=1958-001A

Soviet Union reaches the moon with Luna 2

Septermber 12, 1959

The Soviet Union achieved their goal of making the first hard landing on the moon, impacting the Luna 2 spacecraft into its surface.

Soviet Union safely sends a man to space

April 12, 1961

The Soviet Union achieves a sought triumph in the space race, and becomes the first country to send a man into space, landing safely after 2 hours.

https://www.bbc.co.uk/newsround/56718196

Alexie Leonov takes the first spacewalk

March 18, 1965

The Soviet Union makes another advancement and sends a man into the vacuum of space, completing the first ever space walk, which lasted 12 minutes.

America puts the first man on the moon

July 20, 1969

The United States claims a victory in the space race and makes history by being the first country to put a man on the moon, being televised to 723 million people.

https://www.rmg.co.uk/stories/topics/apollo-11-crew

Soviet Union launches the first space station

April 19, 1971

The Soviet Union took the first steps in creating a space station, which would later become the first parts of the International Space Station 30 years later.

First international, extraterrestrial handshake is made

July 15, 1975

With tensions between the US and Soviet Union easing, the first cooperative mission “Apollo-Soyuz” is launched. The two commanders dock in space and shake hands, symbolically ending the space race.
